Query expansion (claude-haiku)

Staff phrase questions colloquially; the handbook doesn't. Three quick rewrites bridge the vocabulary gap. The original question always keeps the loudest vote in retrieval.

Hybrid retrieval — keyword + semantic, fused

🔤 BM25 keyword arm

    🧭 Vector (semantic) arm

      Legal questions hinge on exact tokens ("Form VX-9", "s.72"); keyword search catches what semantic search fuzzes past — and vice versa. Reciprocal-rank fusion merges both rankings.

      Answer drafting (claude-sonnet)

      The model must answer from the retrieved extracts only, citing paragraph and page for every statement — or refuse with one exact sentence.

      🛡️ The grounding gate — every citation checked

      Each cited (paragraph, page) must match a chunk that was actually retrieved. This is the fabricated-locator safeguard: an invented locator has nothing to match, so it cannot pass. (It proves the citation resolves to retrieved text — whether that text supports the claim is the reader's judgment, at the cited page.)

      Built for the Claude Corps Fellowship · The four outcomes shown here (verified / partial / withheld / refusal) are the grounding gate's complete output vocabulary — it has no path that shows an unchecked answer.
